Lago Di Garda

Italy's jewel nestled amidst the majestic Alps

Destinations

Explore the enchanting towns and villages that grace the shores of Lake Garda. From the medieval charm of Sirmione with its iconic Scaligero Castle and thermal baths, to the bustling streets of Riva del Garda, a haven for water sports enthusiasts, each destination offers its own distinct allure. Discover the colorful harbor of Malcesine, crowned by the majestic Scaliger Castle, or wander the narrow alleys of Bardolino, famed for its vineyards and wine festivals. With a variety of accommodations ranging from luxury resorts to cozy bed-and-breakfasts, there's something to suit every traveler's preference.

Activities

Dive into a world of adventure at Lake Garda, where an array of activities awaits. Feel the thrill of the wind in your sails as you try your hand at sailing or windsurfing on the crystal-clear waters, or paddle along scenic coastlines on a kayak or stand-up paddleboard. Lace up your hiking boots and explore the network of trails that crisscross the surrounding mountains, offering breathtaking views of the lake below. For those seeking a more leisurely pace, cycling routes wind through picturesque villages and vineyards, inviting exploration at your own pace.

Cuisine and Wine

Indulge your taste buds in the flavors of Lake Garda's culinary scene, where fresh ingredients and traditional recipes come together to create unforgettable dining experiences. Sample local specialties such as risotto al pesce Persico (perch risotto), polenta e osei (polenta with small birds), or homemade pasta dishes infused with seasonal ingredients. Pair your meal with a glass of regional wine, whether it's the crisp and citrusy Lugana, the fruity and aromatic Bardolino, or the robust and velvety Valpolicella. Be sure to visit local trattorias, osterias, and agriturismi to savor the true essence of Italian cuisine amidst the beauty of Lake Garda

Attractions

Immerse yourself in the rich history and natural beauty of Lake Garda's top attractions. Step back in time as you wander the ancient halls of Scaligero Castle in Sirmione, or ascend to the heights of Malcesine's Scaliger Castle for panoramic views of the lake and beyond. Explore the lush landscapes of Parco Natura Viva, home to a diverse array of wildlife species, or stroll through the botanical wonders of the Andre Heller Botanical Garden. Thrill-seekers can unleash their inner child at Gardaland, Italy's largest amusement park, while culture enthusiasts can delve into the art and artifacts of the Vittoriale degli Italiani in Gardone Riviera.
